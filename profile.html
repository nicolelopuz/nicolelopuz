GlobeOne Profile

Works GlobeOne Profile

GlobeOne is a go-to digital companion for everything Globe - making you do more with your accounts and all within reach.

Defining the Problem

Needs and Pain Points
  • Navigation can be confusing.
  • Some customers have trouble discerning the difference between account list, account management, account details, and plan details.
  • Customers need to feel secure about the information displayed and those that they share.
Assumptions and Constraints
  • We are assuming that customers manage multiple accounts one at a time. We would need more data on their actual behavior.
  • Editing of account information online is still currently prohibited
  • Customers are wary about sharing information which makes personalization difficult.
Experience Goals
  • We want to create an Account Management experience that is clear, easy to understand, and effortless to navigate.
  • We want our customers to feel secure whenever they share their information with us and give them more agency in modifying preferences and settings.
Experience Principles
  • Knowing - Our users should feel that we know them by auto-populating information.
  • Trust & Safety - Our users should be able to confidently share their information to curate a 1 to 1 experience.
  • Relevant - Our users should see relevant content based on their interests.
  • Familiarity - Our users should be able to complete tasks without any fuss.
  • Empathy - Our users should feel that our intentions are clear and we help them “lessen bill excess charges”

Trend analysis

Ideating Solution

Consistency and standards. Users should not have to wonder whether different words, situations, or actions mean the same thing.

  • Account settings holds GlobeOne information.
  • My Plan contains Globe information such as contract renewal, plan components, subscribed addOns and other plan related data.

Simplify choices for the user by breaking down complex tasks into smaller steps. - Hick’s Law

  • Separate function of add, remove and edit.
  • To edit select on account.
  • Brand identifier such as “Sam’s Postpaid” so when the user types on nickname Sam or Account 1 the system automatically adds post-fix “Postpaid.”

Secure design ensures that users’ sensitive information is protected.

  • It’s important to clearly explain to our users why we asking for their data and how we will used it.

Maslow’s hierarchy of needs, the idea of design rests on the assumption that in order to be successful, a design must meet basic need before it can satisfy higher-level needs.

  • Granular content personalization or preference. Giving the user control over relevant content or even the type of login security will make a happy user and most likely more retention of users.

Metrics

Task success

Efficiency, effectiveness and error rate

  • Locate account management, account details, plan details.
  • Modify plan packs.
  • Switch between multiple accounts.
  • Add or remove accounts in account list.

Retention

The rate of which existing users are returning

  • No. of active users remaining over time.
  • Renewal rate or failure to return (churn).
  • Repeat purchases or continue subscriptions.

Adoption

Gaining more users of a product or feature

  • Maximize plan
  • Purchase new subscription or addOns.

Product description

Words used to describe the product.

  • It can highlight the negative words that can be useful as pain points indicator and areas of improvement.

Summary & Recommendations

  • Align with users mental model on how they expect features and content to be structured.
  • Differentiate customers account: Globe customers plan information vs. GlobeOne users information account management.
  • Precautions on sensitive information such explaining the purpose of collection. A recommendation as well is to have periodic refresh of password and enable two-step authentication.

Opportunity areas to explore

  • Prototype notifications and get more insights.
  • My Plan information
  • Family Base or community data sharing
  • Discover Globe Life Personalization

Conclusion

Users were able to set profiles with multiple accounts. They were able to identify profile as information for the GlobeOne app and not as Globe account information.
